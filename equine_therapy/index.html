What is Equine Therapy?

Equine therapy is a method of psychotherapy where a licensed clinician/equine therapist (i.e., counselor, therapist, or social worker trained in equine therapy) incorporates the use of horses in sessions. They guide clients through various activities with the horses to help address and overcome life challenges. Clinicians often work alongside an equine specialist to provide a safe and beneficial environment for both clients and horses in this experiential form of therapy. Equine therapy offers many unique benefits.

Who May Benefit from Equine Therapy?

Equine therapy is best for individuals ages 10 and older who are seeking assistance in overcoming life challenges. Group therapy sessions can also be valuable for providing immediate feedback on dynamics within the group. Some circumstances may include:

  • Understanding and navigating family relationships
  • Individuals seeking to overcome life challenges
  • Helping youth in similar circumstances learn about themselves and others
  • Team building for organizations
  • and more

No previous horse experience is needed.
